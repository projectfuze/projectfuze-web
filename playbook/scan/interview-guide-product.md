# Interview Guide — Head of Product / PM

**What**: 45-minute structured interview with the product lead or senior PM.
**When**: Week 1, Mon–Tue of the Scan.
**Who**: David conducts. Recorded (with permission) for AI processing.
**Output**: Structured transcript → themes, pain points, contradictions, quotable moments.

---

## Setup

- Duration: 45 minutes
- Recording: Yes (remind at start)
- Tone: Peer-to-peer. You're both product people. Be direct.
- Goal: Understand the decision flow, spec quality, and where alignment breaks between product intent and engineering execution.

---

## Interview Flow

### Opening (3 min)

"I'm here to understand how product decisions actually flow through your org — from idea to shipped feature. I'm not auditing your work. I'm looking for where the system helps you and where it gets in the way."

### Section 1: Decision Flow (12 min)

1. **"Walk me through how a feature goes from 'we should build this' to 'it's in the spec.'"**
   - Listen for: structured process vs. ad-hoc, who's involved, time from idea to spec

2. **"Where do product decisions get documented? Could a new PM find the rationale for any recent decision?"**
   - Listen for: PM tool, docs, Slack, meetings, founder's head

3. **"How do you prioritize? What's the framework, and does the team trust it?"**
   - Listen for: real framework vs. "the founder decides," team buy-in

4. **"What happens when priorities change mid-sprint?"**
   - Listen for: chaos, structured re-prioritization, or "it doesn't happen" (it does)

### Section 2: Specs & Handoffs (12 min)

5. **"Show me a recent spec you're proud of. What makes it good?"**
   - Listen for: clarity, structure, completeness — and whether engineering would agree

6. **"Now show me one that didn't go well. What happened?"**
   - Listen for: misinterpretation, scope creep, missing context, vague requirements

7. **"When engineering has a question about a spec, what happens?"**
   - Listen for: Slack ping, meeting, ticket comment — how much context-switching

8. **"How often does what ships match what was specced?"**
   - Listen for: percentage, frustration level, "we've accepted some drift"

### Section 3: Alignment (10 min)

9. **"How aligned are you with engineering on what 'done' means for a feature?"**
   - Listen for: shared definition vs. different expectations

10. **"What's your relationship with design? How does design intent survive to production?"**
    - Listen for: handoff quality, design system maturity, interpretation gaps

11. **"If you could change one thing about how Product and Engineering work together, what would it be?"**
    - Listen for: the real friction point

### Section 4: AI & Tools (5 min)

12. **"How are you using AI in your product work today?"**
    - Listen for: research, spec drafting, analysis — or not at all

13. **"If AI could draft specs from a structured brief, what would you do with the freed-up time?"**
    - Listen for: strategic work they're not doing because they're writing specs

### Closing (3 min)

14. **"What keeps you up at night about how your team operates?"**

15. **"Anything I should ask engineering that you can't?"**
    - Listen for: the unspoken tension

## Post-Interview Processing

Feed recording to AI agent with prompt:
1. Structured transcript with timestamps
2. Key themes (max 5)
3. Decision flow map (as described by this person)
4. Spec quality assessment (from examples discussed)
5. Handoff pain points (explicit and implicit)
6. Contradictions with founder interview (if completed)
7. Quotable moments
