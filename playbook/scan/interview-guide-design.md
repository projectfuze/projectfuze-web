# Interview Guide — Design Lead

**What**: 30-minute structured interview with the design lead (if the role exists).
**When**: Week 1, Mon–Tue of the Scan.
**Who**: David conducts. Recorded (with permission) for AI processing.
**Output**: Structured transcript → design intent gaps, interpretation issues, handoff quality.

---

## Setup

- Duration: 30 minutes
- Recording: Yes
- Tone: Collaborative. Designers often feel caught between Product and Engineering.
- Goal: Understand how design intent survives through the pipeline — from brief to production.

---

## Interview Flow

### Opening (2 min)

"I'm looking at how intent — the 'what we mean' — travels through your org from decision to shipped feature. Design is where intent gets visual, so I want to understand where that translation works and where it breaks."

### Section 1: Design Process (10 min)

1. **"When you get a brief or spec, what does it typically look like? What's usually missing?"**
   - Listen for: spec quality from the design perspective, what context is absent

2. **"How do you know when a design is 'done'? What makes you confident it communicates the right intent?"**
   - Listen for: design review process, stakeholder alignment, gut vs. system

3. **"Do you have a design system? How comprehensive is it?"**
   - Listen for: component library maturity, consistency, whether it constrains or just suggests

### Section 2: Handoff to Engineering (10 min)

4. **"Walk me through a recent handoff to engineering. What did you give them?"**
   - Listen for: Figma link only vs. annotated specs, interaction details, edge cases

5. **"How often does the built version match the design? Where does it drift?"**
   - Listen for: specific pain points — spacing, interactions, edge cases, responsive behavior

6. **"When engineering interprets something differently than you intended, how does it get resolved?"**
   - Listen for: back-and-forth cycle, time cost, frustration level

7. **"What would the ideal handoff look like?"**
   - Listen for: their vision of structured, complete handoffs

### Section 3: AI & Design (5 min)

8. **"How are you using AI in your design work?"**
   - Listen for: exploration, variants, prototyping, or not at all

9. **"If AI agents could generate UI from structured design specs, what would need to be true about those specs?"**
   - Listen for: constraints, component rules, intent documentation

### Closing (3 min)

10. **"What's the biggest thing that gets lost between what you design and what ships?"**
    - Listen for: the core frustration — this often reveals the handoff break

---

## Post-Interview Processing

Feed recording to AI agent with prompt:
1. Structured transcript with timestamps
2. Design process maturity assessment
3. Handoff quality: design → engineering
4. Design system maturity level
5. Interpretation gap examples
6. AI readiness of design function
7. Quotable moments
